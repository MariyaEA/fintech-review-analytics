# Task 4: Insights and Recommendations

## Overview

This section summarizes the key customer experience findings from Google Play Store reviews of three Ethiopian mobile banking applications:

- Commercial Bank of Ethiopia (CBE)
- Bank of Abyssinia (BOA)
- Dashen Bank

The analysis combines sentiment classification and TF-IDF keyword extraction to identify customer satisfaction drivers, operational pain points, and product improvement opportunities.

# Cross-Bank Sentiment Summary

A total of 1,500 reviews were analyzed.

| Bank | Negative | Neutral | Positive |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| BOA | 211 | 9 | 280 |
| CBE | 147 | 7 | 346 |
| Dashen | 181 | 6 | 313 |

Overall, positive sentiment was dominant across all banks. However, negative reviews reveal recurring reliability and authentication concerns.

---

# Commercial Bank of Ethiopia (CBE)

## Satisfaction Drivers

1. Convenience and accessibility
2. Transfer usability and digital banking access

## Pain Points

1. Login instability
2. Transaction delays

## Recommendations

1. Improve authentication reliability and OTP consistency
2. Strengthen transaction monitoring and error recovery systems

---

# Bank of Abyssinia (BOA)

## Satisfaction Drivers

1. Interface simplicity
2. Mobile banking convenience

## Pain Points

1. OTP verification failures
2. Highest negative sentiment concentration among the three banks

## Recommendations

1. Prioritize OTP infrastructure improvements
2. Improve customer support workflows for access-related issues

---

# Dashen Bank

## Satisfaction Drivers

1. App usability
2. Banking accessibility and convenience

## Pain Points

1. Application crashes and instability
2. Network-dependent transaction interruptions

## Recommendations

1. Improve application stability monitoring
2. Optimize backend transaction handling and retry systems

---

# Common Product Improvement Themes

Across all three banks, the most common operational concerns include:

1. Authentication reliability
2. OTP delivery consistency
3. Transaction performance
4. Application stability
5. Clearer error messaging

---

# Business Implications

The findings demonstrate that customer satisfaction in Ethiopian fintech applications is strongly linked to operational reliability. Customers generally respond positively to convenience and digital accessibility, but negative sentiment increases significantly when login, transfer, or stability issues prevent successful task completion.

Improving reliability therefore represents both a technical priority and a customer retention strategy for Ethiopian banks.
